Bangalore

Bangalore (Bengaluru) is the capital of Karnataka State. It is located in the south eastern part of the Karnataka. Situated at a height of 949 meters (3113 ft.) above the sea level, the city is blessed with pleasant climate throughout the year. Bangalore has been Ranked most Dynamic city in the world. Bangalore called as Silicon Valle of India, it has has highest number of IT companies in India. Bangalore also called as Garden city of India because of its beautiful gardens. It is India's third most popular city, Bangalore is the second-highest literacy rate among the metropolitan cities in the nation. Bangalore has developed into one of India’s major Economic hubs and it is second fastest-growing major metropolis in India, it is India's fifth largest city . It is a multi-cultural city permeating class, religion and language. The City has most well-recognized Universities, colleges, IT companies, research institutions, public sector, Heavy Industries, Aerospace and Defense organizations in India.

History

Kempegowda I was the founder of Bangalore in 1537 AD. Early name was Bendha Kaalooru [Bendha Kaalu means Boiled Beans in Kannda] [ooru means City]. Later it became Bengaluru in Kannada, Bangalore in English. Kempegowda built a mud fort in city market area, balepete, chikpete,Cottonpet etc. Kempe Gowda's son built a four watch towers to mark the city. Today Bangalore has extended beyond the these four towers. Present city build by Kempegowda by the help of Vijayanagar Kingdom King Achutaraya. A feudal lord himself, he used to serve under the Vijayanagara Kings. In the year 1638, Shahajirao Bhonsle, father of Shivaji, captured the city. In 1687, Aurangzeb's army captured Bangalore and sold it to the Wodeyars. The Wodeyars then built the famous Lal Bagh in 1759. After the death of Krishnaraja Wodeyar II in 1759, Hyder Ali, Commander-in-Chief of the Mysore Army took the incharge of Bangalore. The kingdom later passed to Hyder Ali's son Tippu Sultan, known as the Tiger of Mysore. He built the Tippu Sultan summer palace in the heart of the city KR Market. He made Bangalore an army town. He died in 4th Anglo-Mysore war in 1799. British took completely control of Bangalore. In 1809 the British Cantonment was established, and in 1831 the British moved their regional administrative base from Srirangapatnam to Bengaluru. They introduce Railway, Postoffice, Telegraph. In 1881 British returned city to Wodeyar. Bangalore is the first city to have electric power street light in Asia continent in the year 1905. Bangalore's rule changed hands several times after the fall of the Vijayanagara Empire.

TRANPORTATION

Bangalore, or Bengaluru, is a city in southern India. Here, you’ll find modern, affordable transportation options, including the Buses, Auto, Metro, Taxis, and Trains. The public transport in the city ranges from the ordinary buses to the much expensive city taxis.

Bus

Bangalore Metropolitan Transport Corporation (BMTC) is a government agency that operates the public transport bus service in Bangalore. It has the highest number of Volvo buses operated by a public transport company in India.

BMTC is the best and cheapest mode of transportation in Bangalore. It has very good network of routes connecting all the significant places of the city. Most of the people are dependent on BMTC. Travelling arround the city by buses is the cheapest and one the most convenient ways. The BMTC buses are affordable and an individual has both AC and Non-AC bus option.

Interestingly, Bangalore has the highest number of Volvo buses as compared to any other city in the nation. BMTC has arround 900 AC Volvo buses running across the city.

Buses display the route number in English while the distination of the bus and route information displayed in kannada. It connects to every corner of city, with frequent and good service.

It provides several kind of services like Ordinary, Suvarna, Pushpak, JNurm, Atal Sarige, Vajra (Volvo), Vayu Vajra. Vayu Vajra was introduced to operate the bus service from Bangalore International Airport to different parts of city.

Metro

Bangalore Metro Rail Corporation Limited (BMRCL) is second largest Operational Metro network in India.

Namma metro Ranked 83rd largest metro system in terms of length and 79th largest metro network in terms of operating stations in the world.

Bangalore Metro is a safe and efficient way to get to different parts of the city. East-West Corridor is named Purple Line. North-South Corridor is named Green Line and also first underground metro line in SouthIndia. The Green Line, connecting Nagasandra in the north and Yelachenahalli in the south, spans a 24.22 km stretch. With 24 stations including terminals, the entire journey on the Green Line can be covered in 45 minutes including stoppage.

The Purple Line, which connects Byappanahalli in the west and Mysore Road in the east, can be covered in 35 minutes including stoppage.

The metro service runs between 5 a.m. to 11 p.m. daily. Namma Metro is gradually increasing its reach, growth and development. Average speed of namma metro is 35km/h and top speed is 90 km/h.

The Namma Metro provides the option of Smart Tokens and Smart Cards for travel. Namma Metro Travel Card or Contact-less Smart Cards(CSC) has been introduced to make daily commuting easier and cheaper. These tokens are available for a minimum cost of Rs. 10. Each token is valid for a single journey.

The train frequency during peak and off peak hours will vary from 4 min to 20 min. Trains and stations are CCTV fitted with visual and audio service information. Passengers have emergency voice communication with train staff.

Tickets are not required for children below 3 feet. No age criteria is considered.

Autorickshaw

The first autorickshaw to serve Bangalore commenced operations in December 1950. One of the simplest and best ways to travel around the city is in an auto rickshaw. Autorickshaws are easily available throughout the city at all hours of the day.

Autorickshaw is an alternative for City Taxi in Bangalore. Always insist on going by the meter and do not even pay a rupee extra amount that is displayed in the meter. If the meter does not work, fix the fare in advance. Normally their fare rates of these autos controlled by the government.

The minimum meter fare is Rs 25 for first 1.9 Km. Per km fare 1.9 km is Rs. 13. Night meter fare 1.5 times between 10pm and 5am. The driver's information will be displayed in the Auto Rickshaws.

In some places, the auto rickshaws run on shared basis. In case of shared auto rickshaws, the fares are cheaper.

Apart from the main meal, there are many other tasty local foods like idli, dosa, kesari bath, khara bath, bise bele bath.
