Bangalore

Bangalore (Bengaluru) is the capital of Karnataka State. It is located in the south eastern part of the Karnataka. Situated at a height of 949 meters (3113 ft.) above the sea level, the city is blessed with pleasant climate throughout the year. Bangalore has been Ranked most Dynamic city in the world. Bangalore called as Silicon Valle of India, it has has highest number of IT companies in India. Bangalore also called as Garden city of India because of its beautiful gardens . It is India's third most popular city. Bangalore has the second-highest literacy rate among the metropolitan cities in the nation. It is the second fastest-growing major metropolis in India. It is a multi-cultural city permeating class, religion and language. Bangalore has most well-recognized Universities, colleges, IT companies and research institutions in India.

History

Kempegowda I was the founder of Bangalore in 1537 AD. Early name was Bendha Kaalooru [Bendha Kaalu means Boiled Beans in Kannda] [ooru means City]. Later it became Bengaluru in Kannada, Bangalore in English. Kempegowda built a mud fort in city market area , balepete, chikpete,Cottonpet etc. Kempe Gowda's son built a four watch towers to mark the city. Today Bangalore has extended beyond the these four towers. Present city build by Kempegowda by the help of Vijayanagar Kingdom King Achutaraya. A feudal lord himself, he used to serve under the Vijayanagara Kings. In the year 1638, Shahajirao Bhonsle, father of Shivaji, captured the city. In 1687, Aurangzeb's army captured Bangalore and sold it to the Wodeyars. The Wodeyars then built the famous Lal Bagh in 1759. After the death of Krishnaraja Wodeyar II in 1759, Hyder Ali, Commander-in-Chief of the Mysore Army took the incharge of Bangalore. The kingdom later passed to Hyder Ali's son Tippu Sultan, known as the Tiger of Mysore. He built the Tippu Sultan summer palace in the heart of the city KR Market. He made Bangalore an army town. He died in 4th Anglo-Mysore war in 1799. British took completely control of Bangalore. In 1809 the British Cantonment was established, and in 1831 the British moved their regional administrative base from Srirangapatnam to Bengaluru. They introduce Railway, Postoffice, Telegraph. sssIn 1881 British returned city to Wodeyar. Bangalore is the first city to have electric power street light in Asia continent in the year 1905. Bangalore's rule changed hands several times after the fall of the Vijayanagara Empire.

Tourist Places

  • Lalbhag

    • Lalbagh Botanical Garden is located in Bangalore. A haven for all nature lovers, Lal Bagh covers an area 240 acres in the heart of the city and has nearly 1,854 species of plants. It was commissioned by Hyder Ali in 1760 and completed by his son Tipu Sultan. The garden features rare plants of French, Persian and Afghani origin. It comprises the largest collection of India’s rare tropical plants, many ancient trees.

  • Cubbon Park

    • The park was originally spread over 100 acres, which was later extended to 300 acres. Cubbon Park was created by the British Chief Engineer of Mysore, Major General Richard Shankey in the year 1870. This beautiful park showcases more than 6,000 species of trees and plants.There are a lot of statues like that of Queen Victoria, King Edward VII, Major General Sir Mark Cubbon, Rajya Dhurandhara Sir K. Sheshadri Iyer and Sri Chamarajendra Wodeyar. There is a best children’s parks in Cubbon park. There is a small toy train inside the park.

TRANPORTATION

Bangalore, or Bengaluru, is a city in southern India. Here, you’ll find modern, affordable transportation options, including the Buses, Auto, Metro, Taxis, and Trains. The public transport in the city ranges from the ordinary buses to the much expensive city taxis.

Bus

BMTC is the best and cheapest mode of transportation in Bengaluru. It has very good network of routes connecting all the significant places of the city. BMTC Buses (Bangalore Metropolitan Transport Corporation) are the best public transportation. Most of the people are dependent on BMTC. BMTC has arround 900 AC Volvo buses running across the city. Travelling arround the city by buses is the cheapest and one the most convenient ways. Buses display the route number in English while the distination of the bus and route information displayed in kannada. It connects to every corner of city,with frequent and good service. It provides several kind of services like Ordinary,Suvarna,Pushpak,JNurm,Atal Sarige,Vajra (Volvo),Vayu Vajra. Vayu Vajra was introduced to operate the bus service from Bangalore International Airport to different parts of city.

Metro

Bangalore Metro Rail Corporation Limited (BMRCL) is second largest Operational Metro network in India. Namma metro Ranked 83rd largest metro system in terns of length and 79th largest metro network in terms of operating stations in the world. Bangalore Metro is a safe and efficient way to get to different parts of the city. East-West Corridor Is Named Purple Line. North-South Corridor Is Named Green Line And also first underground metro line in SouthIndia. Available from 5 am to 11 pm .Average speed of namma metro is 35km/h and top speed is 90 km/h. Bangalore metro rail is by and large one of the cheapest modes of transport in the city.The Bangalore Metro provides the option of Smart Tokens and Smart Cards for travel. The train frequency during peak and off peak hours will vary from 4 min to 20 min. Trains and stations are CCTV fitted with visual and audio service information. Passengers have emergency voice communication with train staff. Tickets are not required for children below 3 feet. No age criteria is considered. Daily passengers 1.9 lakh and Daily earning 39 lakh

Autorickshaw

One of the simplest and best ways to travel around the city is in an auto rickshaw. Auto-rickshaws are easily available throughout the city at all hours of the day. Autorickshaw is an alternative for City Taxi in Bangalore.

Taxi

You can hire a car or taxi from the airport, the railway station or one of the car rental agencies in the city. Hired cars or taxis are a good way to move around the city and explore all that it has to offer. However, parking might be a problem in some of the crowded market areas.

LIFESTYLE

Culture

The city is visited by people from all corners of the world. The Bangalore culture is a mix of several Indian cultures. This city is rich in several forms of art, music, dance and drama. The carnatic music is a famous form of Indian music. Bangalore is an important center for Indian classical dance and music. Kannada is the offical language of Karnataka state, Kannada language spoken by majority of Bangalureans. Apart from Kannada ,English also widely spoken in the city. The main festivals celebrated in the city are Ganesha chathurthi, Dasara, Dipawali, Ugadi, christmas. One of the major religions of Bangalore is Hinduism. Apart from that, a number of other religions are also practiced in the city. Bangalore city is the center of Kannada film industry also called as Sandal Wood. Kannada movies releases nearly more than 100 movies per year. The pubs, nightclubs as well as the party have become a part of Bangaoreans life. Bangalore has highest number of pubs in Asia.

Food

Bangalore street food is famous for its taste, range and sheer affordability. Bangaloreans love to eat street food and stalls at every corner of the street testify to the fact. Most preferred street foods are spicy chats like bhel puri, masala puri, sev puri, pav bhaji and tangy pani puri. Apart from chats, you can also savor delicious idli, dosa, biryani, sweets and more. The main meal of Karnataka usually consists of rice, saaru or rasam, chapatti, vegetable curry, curd and buttermilk. Apart from the main meal, there are many other tasty local foods like idli, dosa, kesari bath, khara bath, bise bele bath. Bangalore is famous for South Indian food, apart from south indian north indian, chinese food also famous in Bangalore.
